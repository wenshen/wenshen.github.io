About Me

I am a PhD student affiliated with the MONDEGO group at the University of California, Irvine. My advisor is Prof. Cristina V. Lopes. I received my M.Sc in Computing and Information Science from Masdar Institute of Science and Technology in Abu Dhabi, and B. Research

I study interaction design to achieve desirable objectives in dynamic environments. My research interests lie generally in the areas of distributed artificial intelligence, interactive machine learning, algorithmic economics, and human-machine interaction. My current work focuses on: (1) information/mechanism design to induce cooperation among agents with different interests for online platforms (e.g., ridesharing, crowdfunding, crowdsourcing); (2) learning in sequential decision making problems with uncertainty; (3) design and development of scalable simulation frameworks for modeling cooperative social-technological systems.

I have participated in the following research projects:

  • Sequential Decision Making with Uncertainty
  • Information/Mechanism Design for Online Platforms
  • Managing Autonomous Mobility on Demand Systems for Social Good (with Cristina V. Lopes and Jacob W. Crandall)
    Paper: PRIMA'15, IJCAI'16
  • Monitoring and Predictive Maintenance of Buildings and Building Systems (with Afshin Afshari and Ke Yan )
    Paper: Energy and Buildings 2015, 2014
  • Tradeoffs in Regulating Highly Automated Machine Ecologies (with Jacob W. Crandall and Iyad Rahwan)
    Paper: Thesis, Working Paper
  • Electricity Demand Time Series Forecasting (with Vahan Babushkin, Zeyar Aung, and Wei Lee Woon)
    Paper: ACM eEnergy'13
