About

I am a 4th-year Ph.D. student in the Information and Computer Science program at the University of California, Irvine, where I have been working with Prof. Cristina Lopes since Fall 2014. Before that, I was a research engineer at Masdar Institute of Science and Technology in Abu Dhabi, UAE. From there, I also received my M.Sc degree in Computing and Information Science. Prior to Masdar, I spent four memorable years at Northwestern Polytechnical University in Xi'an, China.

Research

My research is driven by the following intriguing question: how can we establish, maintain and regulate the interactions between humans and agents (e.g., machines, robots, chatbots, and virtual assistants) for desired outcomes? To answer it, I develop theory and applications that span human-agent interaction, machine learning, game theory, decision theory and network science. My current work focuses on the computational aspects of incentive design to induce cooperation among agents with different (perhaps conflicting) interests for socially desired objectives, with application to online platforms (e.g., ridesharing, crowdfunding) and networks. Typical methodologies include information design, mechanism design, learning from revealed preferences, and multi-agent simulation.
